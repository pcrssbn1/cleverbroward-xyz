Clever Broward: Your Gateway to Broward County School Resources

Understanding the Clever Broward Portal System

Clever Broward serves as the centralized digital hub for Broward County Public Schools, the sixth-largest school district in the United States with over 271,000 students across 241 schools. This single sign-on platform eliminates the frustration of managing multiple usernames and passwords by providing one secure login that opens doors to dozens of educational applications and resources. Since its implementation in 2016, Clever has transformed how students and educators access digital learning tools throughout the district.

The portal connects directly with district authentication systems to verify user credentials and grant appropriate access based on grade level, school assignment, and role. Elementary students see age-appropriate applications like Starfall and ABCya, while high school students access advanced placement resources, career planning tools, and college preparation platforms. Teachers receive administrative access to gradebooks, curriculum planning software, and communication tools that streamline classroom management.

Broward County Public Schools partnered with Clever to address the growing complexity of educational technology management. Before this centralized system, students spent valuable instructional time logging into separate platforms, often forgetting credentials and requiring IT support. The district reported a 67% reduction in password reset requests during the first year of full implementation. This efficiency gain translates to more time spent on actual learning rather than technical troubleshooting.

For detailed information about our frequently asked questions, visit our FAQ page where we address common login issues and troubleshooting steps. The platform integrates seamlessly with the district's existing infrastructure while maintaining compliance with federal student data privacy laws including FERPA and COPPA.

The district continuously evaluates and adds applications based on educator feedback and student outcomes data. According to Broward County Public Schools' 2023 Technology Plan, digital tool adoption increased by 43% since implementing the Clever portal. Login Process and Account Management

Accessing Clever Broward requires district-issued credentials provided during student registration or employee onboarding. Student usernames typically follow a standardized format combining the student identification number with a district domain. New students receive their login information within 48 hours of enrollment processing, either through their school's front office or via email to the parent contact on file.

The login process begins at the official Clever portal where users select their district from a dropdown menu or search for Broward County Public Schools. After selecting the district, users authenticate using one of three methods: manual username and password entry, Google account integration for those with existing district Google accounts, or badge scanning for elementary students using physical Clever Badges. The badge system, implemented in 2018, allows younger students who cannot yet type proficiently to scan a QR code for instant access.

Security measures protect student data through multi-layered authentication and session management. The system automatically logs users out after 30 minutes of inactivity and requires password changes every 180 days for staff accounts. Student passwords must contain at least 8 characters with a combination of letters and numbers. The district's Information Security Office monitors login attempts and flags suspicious activity for immediate investigation.

Password recovery follows strict verification protocols to prevent unauthorized access. Students must contact their school's media specialist or technology coordinator who verify identity using information from the student information system before issuing temporary credentials. Staff members submit password reset requests through the district's IT help desk at https://www.browardschools.com, which processes requests within 2 business hours during school days. Parents seeking assistance should reference the FAQ page for guidance on helping students with login difficulties.

District Technology Infrastructure and Support

Broward County Public Schools maintains one of the largest educational technology infrastructures in Florida, supporting over 180,000 devices including Chromebooks, iPads, and desktop computers across all campuses. The district invested $127 million in technology upgrades between 2020 and 2023, including network infrastructure improvements that increased bandwidth capacity by 400%. This expansion ensures the Clever portal remains accessible even during peak usage periods when thousands of students log in simultaneously.

Technical support operates through a tiered system addressing issues from basic password resets to complex application integration problems. School-based technology staff handle first-level support, resolving approximately 85% of issues without escalation. District technology specialists provide second-tier support for application-specific problems and account provisioning errors. The central IT department manages infrastructure, security, and vendor relationships to ensure continuous portal availability.

The district reports 99.7% uptime for the Clever portal during the 2022-2023 school year, with planned maintenance occurring during weekends and school breaks. When technical difficulties arise, the district communicates through multiple channels including automated phone messages, email alerts to staff, and social media updates on official district accounts. Real-time status information appears on the district technology page at https://www.browardschools.com.

Professional development ensures educators maximize the portal's potential for instructional purposes. The district provides monthly training sessions covering new applications, advanced features, and integration strategies. According to data from the Florida Department of Education, Broward County ranks among the top 5 districts statewide for technology integration in classroom instruction. Summer institutes offer intensive multi-day workshops where teachers earn professional development points while building digital teaching skills.
