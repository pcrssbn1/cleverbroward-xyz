About Clever Broward and Educational Technology in Broward County

The Mission Behind Clever Broward

Clever Broward exists to simplify educational technology access for one of America's largest school districts while maintaining the highest standards of student data security and instructional effectiveness. Broward County Public Schools serves over 271,000 students across 241 schools, making it the sixth-largest school district in the United States and the second-largest in Florida. Managing digital resources for this population requires sophisticated systems that balance ease of use with robust security protocols.

The district adopted the Clever single sign-on platform in 2016 after extensive evaluation of educational technology solutions. Prior to implementation, students and teachers managed separate credentials for each application, leading to lost instructional time, frequent password resets, and inconsistent technology adoption across schools. The transition to a unified portal reduced password-related support tickets by 67% in the first year while increasing digital tool utilization by 43% according to district technology reports.

This website serves as an independent informational resource to help students, teachers, and families understand how to access and maximize the Clever Broward portal. We provide clear explanations of login procedures, application guides, troubleshooting tips, and answers to common questions. While not officially affiliated with Broward County Public Schools, our content draws from official district policies, technical documentation, and established best practices in educational technology implementation.

Our FAQ page offers detailed answers to specific questions about password resets, application access, and technical requirements. The main page provides comprehensive information about available applications, district technology infrastructure, and support resources. Together, these resources empower the Broward County school community to engage confidently with digital learning tools that enhance educational outcomes.

Educational Technology Philosophy and Implementation

Broward County Public Schools embraces educational technology as a tool for equity, ensuring all students regardless of socioeconomic background have access to high-quality digital learning resources. The district's 1:1 device initiative provides Chromebooks to students in grades 3-12 for both school and home use, eliminating the digital divide that previously disadvantaged students without home computers. Elementary students in kindergarten through second grade use shared classroom devices appropriate for their developmental stage and supervision needs.

The application selection process involves rigorous evaluation by curriculum specialists, technology coordinators, and classroom teachers who assess educational value, data privacy compliance, technical compatibility, and alignment with Florida state standards. According to the Florida Department of Education, Broward County consistently ranks among the top districts statewide for effective technology integration in instruction. Applications must demonstrate measurable impact on student learning outcomes before receiving approval for district-wide deployment through Clever.

Professional development ensures teachers possess the skills to integrate digital tools meaningfully into instruction rather than using technology for its own sake. The district provides ongoing training through monthly workshops, summer institutes, online modules, and embedded coaching from instructional technology specialists. This investment in human capacity building recognizes that technology alone does not improve education—skilled educators using appropriate tools in pedagogically sound ways create the conditions for enhanced learning.

Implementation follows a phased approach that pilots new applications with volunteer teachers before expanding to broader audiences. Feedback from these early adopters informs training materials, troubleshooting guides, and usage recommendations. This iterative process reduces disruption while building institutional knowledge about effective practices. The district publishes annual technology reports documenting adoption rates, user satisfaction, technical performance, and return on investment for transparency and continuous improvement.

Privacy, Security, and Compliance Standards

Student data protection remains the highest priority in all Clever Broward operations, with multiple security layers preventing unauthorized access to sensitive information. The district maintains compliance with the Family Educational Rights and Privacy Act (FERPA), which governs the privacy of student education records, and the Children's Online Privacy Protection Act (COPPA), which regulates the collection of personal information from children under 13. All applications accessible through Clever must sign data privacy agreements committing to these federal standards before integration approval.

Clever itself functions as a data intermediary that facilitates secure authentication without storing unnecessary student information. The platform receives only the minimum data required for account provisioning and application access—typically name, grade level, school assignment, and a unique identifier. Sensitive information such as social security numbers, detailed academic records, and family financial data never passes through the Clever system. This data minimization principle reduces risk exposure while maintaining full functionality.

The district's Information Security Office conducts annual security audits of all connected applications, reviewing data handling practices, encryption standards, breach notification procedures, and vendor security certifications. Applications failing to maintain adequate security measures face immediate suspension until deficiencies are corrected. This proactive monitoring protects student information in an era of increasing cybersecurity threats targeting educational institutions. The U.S. Department of Education provides resources on educational data security that inform district policies and vendor evaluation criteria.

Parents and eligible students possess rights to access, review, and request corrections to education records under FERPA provisions. The district maintains clear procedures for exercising these rights through the Student Records Office. Questions about specific data collection practices, application permissions, or privacy concerns should be directed to school administrators who can explain what information is collected, how it is used, and who has access. Transparency in data governance builds trust while ensuring legal compliance and ethical technology use.
